My educational background is in applied mathematics and computational science with research interests in dynamical systems theory and applications to engineering, physical and chemical sciences.
I have a Ph.D. in Engineering Mechanics and a Graduate Certificate in Computational Engineering Science and Mechanics from Virginia Tech, USA. Prior to that, I obtained a B.E. in Civil Engineering from Jadavpur University, India.
I have held post-doctoral positions in engineering at University of Delaware, University of Pennsylvania, Virginia Tech and in mathematics at University of Bristol and Warwick. During these appointments, I have developed computational methods for discovering structures underlying transport in the phase space of nonlinear dynamical systems that arise in fluid mechanics, rigid body dynamics, chemical reactions, and plasma physics.
